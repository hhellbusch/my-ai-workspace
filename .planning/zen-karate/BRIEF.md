# Martial Arts, Zen, and the Way of Working

**One-liner**: A library of essays connecting martial arts philosophy and lifelong practice to productivity, teamwork, and finding happiness in life and work.

## Problem

The connection between Zen Buddhism and martial arts practice runs deep, but it's hard to convey to people who haven't experienced it. The principles — presence, discipline, beginner's mind, non-attachment — sound like platitudes until you've lived them through training. These essays bridge that gap: grounded in the specific, embodied experience of a lifelong karate practitioner (Hayashi-ha Shito-ryu, with roots in the Okinawan tradition) while drawing from martial arts philosophy broadly. They connect classical teachings to the applied world of engineering teams, leadership, and ways of working.

The scope is deliberately wider than one style or tradition. The philosophical threads — how you train, how you teach, how you carry yourself — run through martial arts worldwide. Karate is the primary lens because that's where the author's decades of practice live, but the ideas aren't confined to it.
