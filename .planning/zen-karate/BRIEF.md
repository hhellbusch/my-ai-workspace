# Zen, Karate, and the Way of Working

**One-liner**: A library of essays connecting Zen Buddhist philosophy and lifelong karate practice to productivity, teamwork, and finding happiness in life and work.

## Problem

The connection between Zen Buddhism and martial arts practice runs deep, but it's hard to convey to people who haven't experienced it. The principles — presence, discipline, beginner's mind, non-attachment — sound like platitudes until you've lived them through training. These essays bridge that gap: grounding classical teachings in the specific, embodied experience of a lifelong karate practitioner and connecting them to the applied world of engineering teams, leadership, and ways of working.

## Success Criteria

How we know it worked:

- [ ] Essay 1 ("The Way Is in Training") published in `docs/` — establishes vocabulary and personal anchor
- [ ] At least 3 essays published, forming a coherent reading track in `docs/README.md`
- [ ] Bridge essay connecting martial arts philosophy to engineering culture/ways of working

## Constraints

- Personal voice requires user input — AI can scaffold but cannot fabricate lived experience
- Classical texts are largely offline; research skill works with transcripts, articles, and summaries
- Existing `docs/` conventions (audience/purpose block, AI disclosure, related reading) must be maintained
- Each essay should stand alone while contributing to the series arc

## Out of Scope

- Martial arts technique instruction or rank/style comparisons
- Comprehensive Zen Buddhism survey or doctrinal analysis
- Motivational or self-help framing
- Prescriptive "how to apply Zen to your team" checklists
