# Changelog — Martial Arts, Zen, and the Way of Working

Evolution log capturing *why* the project's scope and framing changed, not just what changed. Git history has the diffs; this captures the reasoning.

---

## 2026-04-17 — Docs reorganized into track subdirectories

**What changed:** Essays moved from flat `docs/` into `docs/ai-engineering/`, `docs/philosophy/`, and `docs/case-studies/`. The ego/AI essay now lives in `docs/philosophy/`. All cross-references updated.

**Why:** The growing number of case studies and the addition of the philosophy track made the flat structure unwieldy. Track subdirectories give each reading track its own index and reading order.

**Documents updated:** `docs/README.md` (rewritten as master index), all essays (relative links), `STYLE.md` (path conventions), `ROADMAP.md` (publish locations), `repo-structure.md`, `cross-linking.md`, `.cursorrules`, `BACKLOG.md`

---

## 2026-04-17 — Ego/AI essay published ahead of sequence

**What changed:** Thread 14 ("Ego, AI, and the Zen Antidote") matured into a published essay at `docs/philosophy/ego-ai-and-the-zen-antidote.md`, ahead of the planned first essay (The Dojo, Open Source, and Ways of Working).

**Why:** The user noticed a direct connection between The Shift's sycophancy section and the Zen research on ego. The source material (Shi Heng Yi transcript, Jesse Enkamp on mushin) was already cached and ready. The connection to the existing AI essay track was immediate and strong. Writing it out of sequence was the right call because the material was ready and the pipeline could handle it.

**Documents updated:** `ROADMAP.md` (noted out-of-sequence publication), `threads.md` (thread 14 marked as published), `docs/README.md`, `BACKLOG.md`

---

## 2026-04-17 — Scope correction: "Funakoshi's karate is still karate"

**What changed:** Personal notes updated to soften the Okinawan-vs-Japanese distinction from "Okinawan is more authentic" to "different flavor, not better or worse."

**Why:** The user's first instinct was to draw a sharp line between Okinawan karate and Funakoshi's adaptation. The second instinct was to correct that — Shotokan is a legitimate tradition, not a lesser one. The project should acknowledge the spectrum without romanticizing one end.

**Documents updated:** `research/zen-karate-philosophy/personal-notes.md` (Training History and Okinawan vs. Modern Karate sections)

---

## 2026-04-17 — Scope broadened from "Zen and Karate" to "Martial Arts, Zen, and the Way of Working"

**What changed:** Project scope widened from karate-specific to martial arts broadly, with Hayashi-ha Shito-ryu karate as the primary lens. Titles, descriptions, and terminology conventions updated across all planning and research documents.

**Why:** The user said: "while my training has been in karate — i would like to perhaps widen it to be martial arts in general — i have been expanding my learnings beyond just japan/okinawa lately." The user's learning was expanding in real time, and the project scope needed to reflect that growth.

**Documents updated:** `BRIEF.md` (title, one-liner, problem), `STYLE.md` (terminology section renamed and expanded), `personal-notes.md` (training history, philosophical anchors), `threads.md` (scope note added), `ROADMAP.md`

---

## 2026-04-17 — Project scaffolded

**What changed:** Initial project created with brief, roadmap, style guide, research workspace (personal notes template, curated reading template), and backlog items.

**Why:** User expressed interest in exploring the connection between Zen Buddhism, karate, and applied philosophy for productivity and teamwork. Shi Heng Yi's teachings were the initial catalyst.

**Documents updated:** `BRIEF.md`, `ROADMAP.md`, `STYLE.md`, `research/zen-karate-philosophy/personal-notes.md`, `research/zen-karate-philosophy/curated-reading.md`, `BACKLOG.md`
