## About

Mo Bitar responds to Demis Hassabis (DeepMind CEO) advising undergrads to skip traditional internships and focus on AI tool proficiency. Mo's counter: the "agentic coding is a skill" framing is temporary and shrinking with every model release; real engineering compounding value is durable. The doctor vs. MRI technician analogy.

---

## The prompt ceiling

> "There is no deeper skill to agentic coding once you start using it. A prompt is ultimately just a prompt. As long as the agent understands what you're saying, it could be a sentence or a whole document specification."

Mo's observation: with Opus 4, he had to write deep specs to get good results. With Opus 4.5, two sentences sufficed. The "spec-writing skill" Karpathy described is collapsing as models get better at intent-resolution. The ceiling on prompt skill tracks the current model's limitations — and those limitations are shrinking.

## The doctor vs. MRI technician analogy

| Role | Analogy in engineering |
|---|---|
| MD (doctor) | Engineer who can code, architect, debug, and use AI to 10x their output |
| X-ray/MRI technician | Person who can prompt well but doesn't understand what's being built |

Both will have jobs. One has significantly more career growth, income ceiling, and resilience.

> "That person not only can work offline when there's no internet connection, but can with an internet connection double, triple, 10x their productivity."

## The FOMO critique

> "When you see someone like a CEO in this context, understand that they have an agenda... It's always on behalf of the corporation they represent."

Hassabis is promoting AI tool adoption for a reason (it benefits Google DeepMind). FOMO-driven content is amenable to demos; demos show the peak, not the median. The advice "forego an internship for AI tool proficiency" assumes the AI proficiency gap is durable — Mo's argument is it isn't.
