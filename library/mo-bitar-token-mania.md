## About

A short, pointed critique of enterprise AI hype and what Bitar calls "token mania" — the organizational obsession with AI token output as a proxy for productivity. Uses the Andreessen/Rogan podcast as a jumping-off point. Accessible, wry, and quotable.

---

## Key themes

### The slot machine trap

People working 20-hour days with AI aren't more productive — they're less efficient, stuck in a "one more prompt" loop. The feeling of being at 88% and needing just one more prompt to get to 98% traps engineers in a slot machine dynamic. The prompt inches you forward by 0.1% each time.

### Token mania

> "Every token is technical debt. And by the end of it, you've produced millions of tokens, and you have to sift through all of them."

Jason Fried (Basecamp) analogy: bragging about how many tokens you produce is like holding the camera shutter down and taking 10,000 photos instead of 2 good ones. Now someone has to review all 10,000. The job becomes "token refining" not software engineering.

### The enterprise sales pitch narrative

Andreessen, Altman, and Amodei sell to enterprises. Enterprises want "more productivity, more automation, less need for fickle humans." The replacement narrative isn't necessarily intentional — it's the pitch that enterprises respond to, so it's the pitch that sticks. The narrative is a side effect of the sales target.

### Business objectives, not tokens

"Your job as a manager is to tell your people what objectives to hit. The objective is not more tokens." Managers pushing AI adoption without clear business objectives are driving the dystopia — they've outsourced the question of what to build to the engineers themselves.

### LLM species as discovered

LLMs are what they are. Tool capability is expanding, but base intelligence isn't changing fundamentally. "You don't look at these aliens saying, imagine how smart they'll be in 5 years." Each new model release is mostly tool-use expansion on the same base intelligence, not a fundamental step toward AGI.

- **Fluency isn't evidence** — the token mania critique is the organizational equivalent of mistaking fluent output for correct output.
- **Culture amplification** — AI amplifies management dysfunction just as it amplifies good engineering practices. Bad objective-setting at scale.
