## Key themes

### The sitting-rising test as longevity signal

Brazilian study (ages 51–80): ability to get up and down from the floor without hands predicted mortality over 10 years. Each point lost on a 10-point scale increased risk of early death by 21%. The test is a proxy for lower body mobility, core strength, and balance.

### Chairs as mobility constraint

A standard chair keeps hips fixed at ~90°. The body stops developing and maintaining the muscles needed for deeper ranges of motion. Ergonomic chairs compound the problem — they make it comfortable to stay in one position even longer. The $500–$1000 ergonomic chair is spending money to sit more.

### Floor sitting as passive built-in mobility

Getting up and down from the floor dozens of times a day develops:
- Hip mobility below 90°
- Ankle and knee mobility
- Core strength (no back support → core engages)
- Better posture

Okinawa blue zone observation: floor-based lifestyle (eating, sleeping on tatami) is one proposed factor in exceptional longevity.

### Practical entry points

- Start with 5–10 minutes of floor sitting while watching TV
- Use a floor chair with back support as a transitional tool
- Zafu cushions to raise height slightly while keeping hips engaged
- Build up gradually; expect creakiness at first
