## Why It Matters

Doris provides the neuroscience framework behind Simple Lucas's focus experiment. His core protocol — boring breaks, inhabit the in-between, single-task everything — is grounded in dopamine receptor resensitization research. What makes him distinctively relevant to the zen-karate project is that **his foundational experience was a silent Zen meditation retreat** (9 days, 16 hours/day of meditation, bland food, no talking/reading/writing). He then reverse-engineered what the retreat did to his brain using neuroscience vocabulary and created a simplified protocol.

This makes him a direct bridge between contemplative practice and modern productivity science. He's doing exactly what the essay series attempts: connecting traditional practice (Zen) to practical application (work/focus), with the mechanism (neuroscience) as the explanatory layer.

His DMN/TPN (default mode network / task positive network) framework gives neurological grounding to mushin — the transition from scattered default-mode awareness to single-pointed task engagement is what practitioners describe as entering no-mind.

## Core Framework

**Three Principles:**
1. **Boring breaks** — Take breaks less stimulating than work (wall staring, napping, stretching). Starves dopamine receptors, resensitizes them, makes work feel rewarding by contrast.
2. **Inhabit the in-between** — Don't fill idle moments with phone/content. Wait in silence, drive without music, sit without scrolling. Cultivates what Doris calls "a monk-like quality of mind."
3. **Single-task everything** — Do one thing at a time. Trains rapid DMN→TPN shifting, which is the neural mechanism of flow entry.

**Key concepts:**
- **Dopamine resensitization** (not detox) — You can't remove dopamine; you recalibrate your sensitivity to it
- **DMN/TPN interplay** — Default Mode Network (disengaged) vs. Task Positive Network (focused). Fast switching between them = easier flow entry
- **"ROI on dopamine"** — How much stimulation do you need to feel rewarded? Lower = better focus capacity
