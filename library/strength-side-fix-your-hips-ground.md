## About

Short practical walkthrough of 5 ground-based positions for hip and lower body mobility. Explicitly follows the floor-sitting lineage — "the ground is open to everyone, it's free." References Katy Bowman (*Move Your DNA*), Kit Laughlin (Stretch Therapy), and Kaddour Ziani (*Seven Postures*).

---

## The orca whale analogy (Katy Bowman)

> "Orca whales in captivity — their dorsal fin starts to collapse because it lacks the natural forces of the ocean. Wild orcas do not collapse. Aren't humans the same?"

Hard ground keeps the body soft; soft chairs remove feedback and induce stiffness. The irony: engineered comfort produces the opposite of resilience.

---

## The 5 positions (with progressions)

| Position | Target | Entry point |
|---|---|---|
| Cross-legged (tailor) | hip + spine mobility | pillow under hips if stiff |
| Seiza (kneeling, feet flat) + toes-extended variant | ankles, knees, hips, first ray | raised hips prop to start |
| Deep squat | hip + ankle mobility, digestion, spine decompression | heel elevation + hand assist |
| 90/90 / pigeon | piriformis, hip flexors | pillow under front hip |
| "Gorilla" (Kaddour Ziani) | full lower-body strength in non-standard positions | isometrics first (10 sec holds) |

**Accumulation target:** work up to 10–20 min/day in the deep squat; integrate across calls and phone time.

---

## Key principle

> "It shouldn't be so comfortable it makes you not want to move. And it shouldn't be so uncomfortable it makes you not want to use the setup."

The goal isn't the position — it's making the body fluent in multiple positions throughout the day.
