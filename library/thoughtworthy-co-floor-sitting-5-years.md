## About

5-year retrospective on a furniture-free home office setup. Glo (former fitness trainer) started floor sitting after a serious ankle and back injury; HL joined later. Honest account of the transition, mistakes made, and a "would we do it again?" verdict (yes).

---

## Key themes

### "Stronger over weaker" — their governing principle

> "We prefer getting stronger over becoming weaker... we prefer being comfortable over being uncomfortable, but only if it doesn't come at the expense of making us weaker."

The same logic applies to barefoot shoes: engineered comfort often trains the body to need that comfort, gradually reducing capacity.

### The transition is real and takes months

First 2–3 months: back fatigue, second-guessing, reliance on back-support floor chair. Gradually reduced use of the floor chair as core and back got stronger. After ~5 years: getting up and down from the floor has become second nature.

### The actual goal: interrupt prolonged sitting

The floor setup is a means to an end, not the end. You can be sedentary on the floor too (bean bag position = functionally equivalent to an ergonomic chair). The goal is to create an environment that **breaks the pattern of prolonged sitting in one position**.

> "It shouldn't be so comfortable it makes you not want to move. And it shouldn't be so uncomfortable it makes you not want to use the setup."

### Practical lessons (5-year retrospective)

- Don't make identical setups for different bodies — desk height is highly personal
- Use a back-support floor chair as a transitional tool; plan to phase it out
- Movement-first: the value is in changing positions, not the specific position
