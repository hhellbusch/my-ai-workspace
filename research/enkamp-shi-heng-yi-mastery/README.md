# Jesse Enkamp × Shi Heng Yi — Mastery in Martial Arts

Research workspace analyzing a conversation between two primary sources for the zen-karate essay series.

## Source

**Video:** [If I Started Martial Arts From Scratch In 2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aq92sWWU4FM)  
**Speakers:** Jesse Enkamp (Karate by Jesse) and Master Shi Heng Yi (35th Generation Shaolin, Headmaster Shaolin Temple Europe)  
**Duration:** 20:18  
**Fetched:** 2026-04-19

## Why This Source

Both speakers appear independently in the zen-karate essay research (`research/zen-karate-philosophy/`). This conversation captures dynamics that solo interviews don't: their direct exchange on ego, the master title, student-teacher accountability, and how traditional and modern martial arts relate. Shi Heng Yi's ego arc — described in his own words — is one of the most usable pieces of first-person testimony in the source corpus.

## Key Themes

- Self-perfection through practice (not perfecting the art)
- Mastery = skill + character + contribution
- The ego trap of the "master" title — Shi Heng Yi's personal account
- "Real masters become invisible" — non-attachment made concrete
- Daily consistency over impulse training
- Traditional and modern as complementary, not competing
