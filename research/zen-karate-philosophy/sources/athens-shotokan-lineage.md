## The Athens Karate Club

One of the oldest karate clubs in the nation. Founded 1970 at Ohio University by Greer Golden. Currently ISKF-affiliated, training at the Athens Community Center. The club's Funakoshi quote: "The ultimate aim of the art of Karate lies not in the victory or defeat, but in the perfection of character of its participants."

## Greer Golden (1937–2019)

Born March 12, 1937, Washington, DC. Grew up in southwestern Florida.

### Training in Okinawa
- 1956: Enlisted in US Air Force, deployed to Okinawa
- Began learning Shotokan from **Noboru Kobayashi** on the Air Force base (had originally been looking for Judo)
- 1957: Competing at JKA tournaments. Awarded "Best Fighting Spirit" at JKA All Japan Championships
- 1960: Extended tour of duty to continue training
- 1961: Promoted to 1st Dan — one of the first Americans promoted by the JKA

### Career in the US
- 1961: Returned to US. Invited by **Hidetaka Nishiyama** to join his Los Angeles dojo
- Trained under **Yutaka Yaguchi**
- Enrolled in the Instructor Trainee Program alongside Ray Dalke and Lester Ingber — modeled on the JKA Instructors Course, full-time, 6 hours/day under Nishiyama and Yaguchi
- 1969: First graduates of the program. Licensed to teach
- 1970: **Nishiyama recommended Golden to establish a karate club at Ohio University.** Relocated to Athens, Ohio. Later established another club at Ohio State University
- Developed relationship with **Teruyuki Okazaki** in Philadelphia, traveling from Athens regularly to train
- 1977: Joined ISKF when Okazaki founded it. Appointed to lead the Mid-American Region
- 1998: Promoted to 6th Dan. Appointed to ISKF Technical Committee
- 2013: ISKF began presenting "Best Fighting Spirit" award in Golden's honor
- February 18, 2019: Died in Columbus, Ohio, aged 81

### Significance
One of the first non-Japanese Shotokan instructors in the US. Great communicator, well-respected by students and peers. Memorial Scholarship established in his honor.

## Hirokazu Kanazawa (1931–2019)

Born May 3, 1931, Iwate Prefecture, Japan. Father was a fisherman who died young. Mother Masue was a major influence.

### Early Training
- High school: keen boxer and judoka (2nd Dan Judo)
- First exposure to karate: watched an Okinawan man (Yamashiro) defeat a 5th Dan judoka police officer in a street fight
- 1951: Enrolled at Nippon University, disappointed with weak karate club. Transferred to **Takushoku University** after seeing their students train
- Joined the strong Shotokan club. Trained 5-6 hours/day. Trained privately at night to close the gap with more experienced students
- **Gichin Funakoshi** would come to the club to teach. Kanazawa was responsible for collecting him from his home

### Competition
- 1957: **Won the 1st All Japan Karate Championship kumite title with a broken wrist.** Broke it 5 days before the tournament. His mother, who had traveled to Tokyo to watch, asked: "Haven't you got other limbs you could use?" Won 4 fights by ippon using only his good hand for blocking and kicks for scoring
- 1958: **First-ever Grand Champion of JKA All Japan Championships.** Won kata title (defeating Mikami) and shared kumite title with Mikami

### JKA and International Work
- 1957: Graduated from the JKA Instructors Course alongside Takayuki Mikami. Instructors included Nakayama, Nishiyama, Kase, Okazaki
- 1961–1963: Chief Instructor in Hawaii. Faced 5 physical challenges from other martial artists; defeated all
- Visited Okinawa, including dojos of Chosin Chibana (Shorin-Ryu founder) and Higa Yuchoku
- 1965: World tour with Kase, Enoeda, and Shirai — US, Germany, Belgium, Holland, France, England, South Africa
- 1966: Chief Instructor of KUGB (British). Promoted to 6th Dan
- 1967: Chief Instructor to German Karate Federation
- 1971: Promoted to 7th Dan. JKA Director, General Manager of International Division

### SKIF
- 1977: Left JKA after political disputes. Had resigned as Director but not as instructor; was dismissed by letter while traveling. "He had never wanted to leave the JKA."
- Founded **Shotokan Karate-do International Federation (SKIF)**
- Grew SKIF to branches in 130+ countries
- 2000: Awarded 10th Dan
- 2012: Retired from active traveling and teaching after decades. Passed leadership to son Nobuaki in 2014
- December 8, 2019: Died

### Teaching Philosophy
"The most important points of my teaching are breathing, movement and timing. But breathing is first... If the breathing is wrong, your body will go wrong – your mind will be wrong also – there is a very strong connection. You control your spirit with your breathing."

Also practiced Tai Chi since 1957 (under Mr Yang). Credited it for his longevity in karate. Studied Okinawan kobudo (sai, tonfa, nunchaku). Keen historian of kata origins.

### Quote (Terry O'Neill)
"He is the perfect specimen of the type of person the art of Karate can develop – there will never be a finer living example of what Karate at its highest level really is."

## Teruyuki Okazaki (1931–2020)

Born June 22, 1931, Fukuoka Prefecture, Japan. Studied judo, kendo, and aikido before karate.

### Training
- 1947: Began karate at **Takushoku University** under **Gichin Funakoshi** and **Masatoshi Nakayama**
- 1953: Graduated. Appointed coach of Takushoku karate team
- 1955: Appointed head of JKA Instructor Training Program (developed with Nakayama and Funakoshi). This program produced Kanazawa, Mikami, and other influential Shotokan leaders

### International Work
- 1961: Sent to US by Nakayama. Established dojo in Philadelphia
- 1963: Founded East Coast Shotokan Karate Association
- 1977: **Founded ISKF (International Shotokan Karate Federation)**. Grew to ~50,000 members across 60+ countries
- Faculty member at Temple University since 1970
- 2007: Promoted to 10th Dan
- 2015: Retired as ISKF Chief Instructor
- April 21, 2020: Died, aged 88

```
├── Masatoshi Nakayama (1913–1987) — JKA Chief Instructor
│   ├── Hirokazu Kanazawa (1931–2019) — SKIF founder, one of Funakoshi's last direct students
│   │   └── Stephen Krauss → Athens club connection
│   ├── Teruyuki Okazaki (1931–2020) — ISKF founder
│   │   └── Greer Golden (1937–2019) — founded Athens Karate Club 1970, ISKF Mid-America
│   └── Hidetaka Nishiyama (1928–2008) — trained Golden, sent him to Ohio
└── Direct teaching at Takushoku University → Kanazawa, Okazaki both studied under him
```
