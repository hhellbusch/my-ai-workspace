## Overview

Teruo Hayashi (1924–2004). Founder of Hayashi-Ha Shito-Ryu-Kai and Japan Kobudo Kenshin-Ryu-Kai. Described as one of the last great Japanese Budo masters. Soke Hanshi 10th Dan Okinawa Kobudo Kenshin-Ryu. JKF Official Master "Hanshi" 9th Dan (awarded 1995).

His philosophy: **"I have not invented any katas, what I have done is give them back the sense of force that they should have."**

## Dojo Yaburi

Hayashi practiced dojo yaburi — walking into a dojo and challenging the top students. If beaten, he asked to become a student. If he won, he moved on. Time-honored tradition in Japanese/Okinawan culture. Became infamous for this practice; many dojos refused his challenges outright. This was how he sought both knowledge and respect from the Okinawans.

## Inoue Connection

In 1964, accepted Yoshimi Inoue as uchi-deshi. Inoue's duties: keep the dojo, office, and toilet clean. Train with Hayashi several hours morning and evening. Help with teaching in the afternoon. Training consisted mainly of kihon and kumite — frequently no-holds kumite. Hayashi was in his early 40s and at peak skill; Inoue was still a teenager. Inoue remained uchi-deshi for ~4 years.

## Style Characteristics

Hayashi-Ha Shito-Ryu blends:
- **Nahate roots** (via Goju-ryu — Seiko Higa)
- **Shurite roots** (via Shorin-ryu — Chosin Chibana; via core Shito-ryu — Kuniba/Mabuni)
- **Ryuei-ryu** (Chinese southern tiger style — Kenko Nakaima)
- **Okinawan Kobudo** (multiple teachers)

Shito-ryu is technically two separate lineages blended — one from Nahate, one from Shurite. Hayashi broadened this further with his Okinawan studies.

## Organizational Roles

- President and Soke of Nippon Karate-do Hayashi-Ha Shito-Ryu-Kai
- Technical Chairman of WUKO/WKF for 10+ years
- Emeritus Chairman of WKF Referee Council
- Advisor of Central Technical Committee, Japan Karate-do Federation
- President, Osaka Prefectural Karate-do Federation
- Director of FAJKO
