## Timeline

- **1946-09-27:** Born in Tottori, Japan (small coastal town in southern Japan).
- **~1961 (age 15):** Bought a book written by Teruo Hayashi and began teaching himself karate.
- **~1962 (age 16):** Started formal Shito-ryu training at Tottori University Karate Club.
- **1964 (age 18):** Traveled to Osaka to train under Teruo Hayashi. Met Hayashi at a police station by chance. After 6 months, became Hayashi's uchi-deshi (live-in disciple) — the only uchi-deshi Hayashi ever had.
- **1964–1968:** Uchi-deshi period. Trained morning and evening with Hayashi, taught afternoon classes, cleaned dojo/office/toilets. Training consisted mainly of kihon and kumite — no-holds-barred sparring with Hayashi (then in his early 40s, at his peak).
- **1968:** Returned to Tottori when father became ill (oldest son obligation). With Hayashi's encouragement, opened a branch dojo — Hayashi-ha Shito-ryu Kai branch in Tottori.
- **1970s–1980s:** Built reputation as elite kata coach. Discovered/trained Mie Nakayama (first major student to achieve world champion status).
- **1982–1986:** Nakayama won 3 consecutive WKF World Championship gold medals.
- **1986:** Chosen as Shito-ryu representative (one of four, representing Japan's four major karate styles) for government-sponsored American tour. Gave the American President's wife self-defense lessons in Washington D.C.
- **1990s–2000s:** Trained succession of world champions across multiple styles: Ryoki Abe, Atsuko Wakai, the Hasegawa Brothers, Hisami Yokoyama, Kasuga Wakabayashi.
- **2004-09-24:** Teruo Hayashi died. Inoue remained loyal to Hayashi-ha throughout Hayashi's lifetime.
- **2005:** After one year of bereavement, founded **Inoue-ha Shito-ryu Keishin-kai**, incorporating his own knowledge and experience.
- **2005:** Rika Usami began training under him.
- **2008:** Antonio Diaz began training under him (introduced by Kiyoshi Yamazaki).
- **2010:** Diaz won WKF World Championships (individual kata). Usami won bronze.
- **2012:** Made history — both Usami (female) and Diaz (male) won individual kata world championships at the same event (Paris). First coach to achieve this.
- **2015-02:** Diagnosed with cancer. Chose to fight privately. Continued teaching until his final days.
- **2015-05:** Died of cancer. Was a known chain smoker — once joked "if you want to learn how to breathe in Suparinpei kata you have to learn how to smoke."
- **Post-2015:** His top students (Usami, Nakayama, Diaz) traveled the world promoting Inoue-ha Shito-ryu. Mie Nakayama passed away in September 2021.
- **Current:** Inoue-ha Shito-ryu Keishin-kai has ~40 affiliated dojos worldwide. Current leadership: Kaicho Yukio Okamura, Soke Masahiro Okamura, based in Tottori.

## The Uchi-deshi Experience (in Inoue's words)

From an interview published on the Inoue-ha Karate official site:

> "I used to clean the dojo, the toilets and the office and then do my training until about lunchtime. After eating I'd do some more training and then the students would come at about four or five in the afternoon, so I'd teach them. When they'd finished training I'd do some more training. Pretty much training all day."

> "Being alone in the office was pretty boring, so I used to do kumite practice with Hayashi Sensei as well. He was about 41 or 42 and very much in his prime. It's pretty scary when you get hit hard at the age of 18 or 19. I thought to myself, 'if I'm going to get hit, I might as well get hit moving forward rather than pulling back... the quicker I get knocked out, the quicker it's over.' But when I did get knocked out, he'd just shout at me 'get up!!!', so I figured it didn't make any difference if I was knocked out or not — either way, we'd just train till it was over."

> "At the time, however much I got punched or kicked, I was still always well up for it. The training was pretty tough, but Hayashi Sensei had a really kind nature. He was pretty strict about manners and never once hit me outside of practice. The great thing about karate is that through training you become stronger."

## Teaching Philosophy

### "If kihon can do, any kata can do"
The core principle. Inoue drilled basics relentlessly — 10-hour days of nothing but kihon for visiting students. His students echo this: Antonio Diaz ("kihon is karate"), Mie Nakayama (1 hour of kihon daily), Sakura Kokumai ("practicing only basics for three days, about 10 hours each day").

### Body Mechanics as Universal Principles
"Your style doesn't matter. As long as you have one head, two legs and two arms, human body mechanics and universal principles of efficient movement are the same across all styles." He attracted and trained students from Shotokan, Goju-ryu, and other styles — not just Shito-ryu.

### Three Principles of Power
1. **Balance** — solid stances, correct trajectories. Power comes from the ground up (not the hips). The hips transmit but don't generate.
2. **Speed** — technique execution in 0.2 seconds (blink speed).
3. **Timing** — execute after establishing your base, not during transitions.

### Natural Breathing
Reject the common teaching of coordinating breath with technique. "Your body knows how to breathe from the moment you are born." Keep mouth slightly open — relaxes jaw, reduces tension. Exception: deep abdominal breathing during slow kata sequences for recovery.

### Kime as Transfer, Not Stop
"Kime is like a car crash" — the car (body) stops but the driver (technique) keeps going. Aim to punch through the target, not stop at it. No deliberate contraction or pull-back at impact. Feeling of expansion, not contraction.

### Kata as Movement Between Poses
"Kata is not A, B, C, D — it's how you get from A to B to C to D." A good coach/referee should look for movement between techniques, not just finishing poses.

### Three Levels of Bunkai
1. **Literal application** — the technique does what it looks like.
2. **Creative application** — using the principles while maintaining kata movements.
3. **Non-classical phase** — transcending kata entirely, using principles in completely free formats.

### "Karate is 99% feeling"
Technical knowledge isn't enough. The practitioner must develop internal sensitivity — feeling the mechanics, the timing, the intention.

### Train Less, Improve More
Quality over quantity. Focus and intentionality in practice.

## Quotes

> "Kihon training, although it may be boring, is extremely important. If the foundations of a house are weak, as the house gets older, problems will arise."

> "Power comes from the ground up. It doesn't come or originate from the hip."

> "Kata is not about the external technique only but about the 'philosophy' and 'meaning' of the actual technique."

> "No paints nor brushes, marbles nor chisels, are needed to make the art of karate, for we are the stuff that kata is made of. It is born in our body, exists in our body and dies in our body. Kata, then, is the most personal of all the arts... it springs from the very breath of life."

> "The goal of karate should not only be self-defense. The art should provide a guide for living."

> "The way of Budo is to practice every day, until you die."

> "A good kata is pretty... but a GREAT kata is EFFECTIVE."

> "You cannot fight gravity! Make gravity your friend!"
