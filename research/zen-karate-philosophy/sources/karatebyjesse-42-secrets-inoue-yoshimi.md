## Context

Jesse Enkamp's notes from private training with Soke Inoue Yoshimi. 42 practical teachings on technique, body mechanics, training philosophy, and coaching methodology. Published by the German Karate Federation.

## The 42 Points

### Body Mechanics and Relaxation

1. **Open your mouth.** Closed jaw → stiff shoulders → tense techniques. Open mouth allows air to flow freely, making techniques relaxed, natural, powerful.
3. **Use your posterior chain.** Animals (dogs, cats, horses) are stronger and faster than humans. They have strong posterior chains (back, hamstrings, glutes, triceps, lats). Focus on those, not "show-off" muscles (abs, pecs, biceps).
5. **Use your heels.** Turn on heels, step from heels, keep weight above heels. This keeps shoulders relaxed and knees unlocked → snappy, quick movements.
7. **Hips level to the floor at all times.** Except rare instances in some kata. Because it's natural — that's how we walk.
9. **Body is a car.** Center (tanden) is the engine. Hands and feet are wheels. Steering wheel is in your head.
11. **Don't relax your knees — relax your hips.** Imagine unhinging the hip joint, letting legs drop from the hip socket. Faster than relaxing knees (which tenses quadriceps).
14. **Shoulder always in front of chest.** Never parallel to it. Same for hikite arm.
18. **In front stance, weight on back leg.** Back leg like bamboo — strong but flexible. Front leg is just support. Back leg controls action and power.
19. **Forget your arms.** Focus on core. Whip your center. Arms will fly out automatically. Initiate the whip action.
20. **Relax pectoral muscles.** Tense chest locks collar bones, tightens shoulder connection → impossible to have relaxed, snappy techniques.
29. **Expand, never contract.** What most people call "kime" (contraction/squeeze) is wrong. Think of kicking a soccer ball — you don't tense at impact. Expand your movement, let it carry through.
31. **Stop a millisecond before you punch.** Movement energy transfers to arm. Like a car crash: car stops (body), passenger flies out (arm). Timing is critical.
34. **The One Leg Principle.** One leg is always the "power leg." Figure out which one in every technique and you'll instantly boost power.
36. **Relaxed scapula = snappy technique.** Shoulder blade is the base of your arm. Must be loose.

### Training Philosophy

2. **Train less. Improve more.** Excessive training is bad for health. Two hours a class, three days a week is optimal.
4. **"Karate is 99% feeling."** Many train hard and go home happy but never improve. To improve: use feelings, spirit, imagination. 99% of improving is connecting body to brain. Tap into senses.
6. **"Smile!"** When Inoue thinks you're tense, he says "smile" not "relax." Psychological trick — hard to be tense when smiling. You perform better when happy.
8. **Work with nature, not against it.** At 68 years and 58kg, anyone can beat him in arm wrestling. But karate isn't just size/strength/age. Older → more wisdom, technique, feeling, experience. Body weakens, other skills take over. Natural progression. Don't fight nature — take advantage of it.
17. **Depth over breadth.** Plunge into the detailed mystery of the micro to understand the macro. Improving one small thing improves many things.
24. **Always ask how and why.** When Inoue was young, questioning was never allowed in the dojo. Now he encourages it like crazy. The secret to staying motivated is continuously asking questions.
37. **"Martial arts support gravity. They have friendship."** Cooperate with gravity. Let it pull you into each stance.
41. **Excuses or progress. Can't make both.**

### Coaching and Teaching

26. **Video can teach patterns, only sensei can teach performance.** It's okay to learn kata patterns from video. But how to perform it requires a teacher.
27. **"In my dojo, we start at 9 in the morning and finish 11 in the evening. Sometimes we forget food."**
28. **Kata is not A, B, C, D — it's how you get from A to B to C to D.** A good sensei/coach/referee should always look for the movement *between* techniques, not just the finishing pose. That separates good kata from bad.
39. **Blame your sensei for sluggishness.** If a sensei counts with a lame voice, it's hard to do your best. A sensei should count with great passion to inspire everyone.
40. **Progression of mastery.** Beginners punch with the hand. Experts punch with shoulder blade. Masters punch with the core.

### Kata and Application

12. **Two kinds of karate.** (1) Martial arts and self-defense. (2) Competition. The best way to improve both: focus on how to use the body efficiently. This is what excites Inoue — no matter who you are, you can always improve body mechanics.
15. **Use impact tools when in doubt.** When wondering about the most efficient technique, use a bag or makiwara and experiment. Which is more powerful? Try.
16. **Block's preparatory move is the real block.** In kata, the "preparatory move" before a block is actually the real block — the step forward is the attack, takedown, or joint manipulation.
21. **Style doesn't matter.** One head, two legs, two arms. Body mechanics and principles of efficient movement are the same across all styles.
22. **Forget balance, focus on speed.** Body is smarter than you think — it instinctively keeps you balanced in hard movements. But not if you keep thinking about it.
25. **0.2 seconds.** That's how fast your techniques should be. A blink.
35. **Stances are harder for Westerners.** Longer legs make kata harder but kumite easier. "Japanese people can't kick as high."
