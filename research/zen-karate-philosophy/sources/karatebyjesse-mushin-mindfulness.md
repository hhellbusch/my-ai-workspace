## Key Ideas

### Mushin defined
Shortened from *mushin no shin* — a Zen expression meaning "mind without mind." The concept of emptiness, the void, nothingness applied to martial arts.

### Funakoshi on emptiness
"As a mirror's polished surface reflects whatever stands before it and a quiet valley carries even small sounds, so must the student of Karate-Do render of their mind empty of selfishness and wickedness in an effort to react appropriately toward anything they might encounter. This is the meaning of kara or 'empty' of Karate-Do." — Funakoshi Gichin

### Mabuni on the void
"When the spirit of Karate-Do (Bu) is deeply embraced / It becomes the vehicle (described as a boat) in which one is ferried / Across the great void to the 'world within' (described as 'Bu'-island)" — Mabuni Kenwa

### The paradox
Trying to think of "nothing" inevitably produces something concrete. If something does not exist, it's impossible to think about it. Labels ("the void," "emptiness," "nothingness," "zero") help us grasp the concept, but labels are limited while the void is unlimited. Putting a name on nothingness is to limit its existence.

### Noise and modern life
In an internet-driven world, lives are cluttered by constant noise. Peaceful silence has become discomfort. Joggers can't run without music. Moments of quietness are considered socially awkward. We've become entrenched in noise and forgotten what it's like to exist in silence.

### Mushin as antidote
During moments of silence and nothingness, "a purer side of ourselves reemerges." In the void, we regain the peace of mind of living in the moment — "the unfamiliarity of living consciously and fully in the present time." The power that comes from moments of silence is unimaginable for most people.
