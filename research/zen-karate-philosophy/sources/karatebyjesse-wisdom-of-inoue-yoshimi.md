## About Inoue Yoshimi

Soke Yoshimi Inoue (8th dan JKF), born 1946 in Tottori, Japan. Taught himself karate from a book at age 15. After high school, traveled to Osaka to study under **Teruo Hayashi**, direct student of Mabuni Kenwa (founder of Shito-ryu). Accepted as uchi-deshi (personal student living in the dojo) for 4 years.

Returned to Tottori when his father got sick. Got a day job, opened his first dojo. Became known for producing some of the finest kata specialists in Japan. Basically the whole national kata team under him for many years — Ryoki Abe, Atsuko Wakai, Mie Nakayama (4x female kata world champion), Rika Usami, Antonio Diaz (male kata world champion), the Hasegawa brothers.

Founded Inoue-ha Shito-ryu (his own organization within Shito-ryu).

## Key Teachings from the Seminar

### Scapula tension/relaxation for power
Individual movements of shoulder and scapula connected for maximum power and speed. Full range of movement coupled with proper relaxation and tension (producing kime).

### Against artificial sounds and stomps
Inoue despises loud grunts and artificial noises in kata. Stay completely silent, breathe naturally. Reason: relaxation. Huffing and puffing tenses chest, changes center of gravity. Stomping disrupts relaxation/balance/power output.

### Hip joint relaxation for stepping
Efficient movement through relaxing in the hip socket and knee joint (rather than kicking away with the back leg). Repeated emphasis: "You cannot fight gravity! Make gravity your friend!"

### Kata is about real meaning
Every great kata coach stresses: although kata is an individual performance, never forget the real meaning and intent of movements. "Kata is a solo representation of a collection of defensive sequences (bunkai) meant to save your ass in a self-defense situation. That is the meaning of kata. And that is what you need to express."

## Lineage Connection

Inoue → Teruo Hayashi → Mabuni Kenwa (Shito-ryu founder). This places Inoue directly in the Hayashi-ha Shito-ryu lineage — the user's own training style.
