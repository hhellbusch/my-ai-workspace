## Timeline

- **1986-02-20:** Born in Tokyo, Japan. Youngest of two children; older brother.
- **1996 (age 10):** Began Goju-ryu karate at Katsushika Dojo near family home, inspired by a female fighter on television. Followed her older brother into karate. Dojo affiliated with Gojuryu Karatedo Shoseikan International.
- **1998 (age 12):** First tournament as a green belt — local dojo competitions.
- **2001 (age 15):** First national-level tournament (national schools championship).
- **2003 (age 17):** Won the All Japan High School Karatedo Championships — first major title.
- **2004:** Enrolled at Kokushikan University (renowned for tough training). Met Yoshimi Inoue (Shito-ryu master).
- **2005:** Began training under Inoue at his dojo in Tottori — 5-hour train ride from Tokyo. Won kata title at 4th World Junior & Cadet Championships, Limassol, Cyprus.
- **2007:** Won first of five All Japan Kata titles (35th All Japan Championships, Budokan, Tokyo).
- **2009:** Second All Japan Kata title. Asian Karate Championships gold.
- **2010:** Gold, Asian Games (Guangzhou). Bronze, WKF World Championships (Belgrade). Third All Japan title.
- **2011:** Dominated international circuit — Paris Open, Salzburg Open, others. Fourth All Japan title. Under Inoue's guidance, became top-ranked kata competitor in the world.
- **2012:** Won Paris Open, Dordrecht Open, Jakarta Open, Istanbul Open. Gold medal, 21st WKF World Karate Championships (Paris) — performed Chatanyara Kushanku. Defeated Sandy Scordo (France) 5-0 in the final. 12,000 spectators gave standing ovation. Fifth All Japan title.
- **2013:** Retired from competition at age 27. Enrolled in Kokushikan University graduate school (sports systems research). Traveled with Antonio Diaz giving Inoue-ha seminars.
- **2015-02:** Named JKF Goodwill Ambassador for 2020 Tokyo Olympics.
- **2015-03:** Graduated from Kokushikan graduate school. Appointed JKF National Team Kata Coach.
- **2015-05-01:** Inoue Yoshimi died of cancer. Usami, Nakayama, and Diaz began traveling the world promoting Inoue-ha Shito-ryu.
- **2018:** First child (son).
- **2021-05:** Appointed Chairman of JKF Player Strengthening Committee (coaching director) — first woman in the position. Replaced Masao Kagawa. Introduced biomechanical analysis with 40+ cameras in 360-degree configurations.
- **2021-08:** Appointed Chief Instructor of Nergy Karate Club (women-only, Harajuku, Tokyo).
- **Current:** Coaches at Kokushikan University, runs the annual Rika Usami Cup tournament, works as commentator at All Japan Championships alongside Ryutaro Araga and Ryo Kiyuna (Olympic medalists). People visit her dojo in Tottori to train — including Olympic champion Sandra Sanchez.

## Style and Training Background

- Started in **Goju-ryu** at age 10.
- Transitioned to **Shito-ryu** under Inoue's influence at Kokushikan University.
- Now practices and promotes **Inoue-ha Shito-ryu Keishin-kai**.

## Relationship with Inoue Yoshimi

Usami met Inoue at Kokushikan University. He recognized her potential — "Rika, you can be world number one." Their training relationship defined her career:

- Traveled 5 hours by train (Tokyo to Tottori) to train with him.
- Inoue focused on individual techniques within kata, kumite integration, physical and mental conditioning.
- Unlike many masters, Inoue "spends a lot of individual time together with me, to make sure I understand each waza very clearly, practicing together with me for long hours."
- For 2012 Worlds preparation: trained 10am to 10pm at Inoue's Tottori dojo alongside Antonio Diaz and other international athletes.
- Usami on Inoue: "He teaches me not only the physical aspects of Karate, but he also helps me improve my mental abilities. It's the spiritual aspect."
- When Usami won 2012 Worlds, Inoue cried tears of joy. He made history as first person to coach both male and female individual kata world champions (Usami and Diaz) at the same championship.

## Philosophy and Approach

### Shin-Gi-Tai (Mind-Technique-Body)
When asked for the 3 most important attributes: "Body, mind and technique." The traditional Japanese concept of shin-gi-tai.

### On Traditional vs. Sport Karate
"You shouldn't consider those two — traditional/contemporary karate — as separate things. Instead, try to find where the vital areas shared by both approaches converge, and focus on those."

### On Kata and Kumite
"There is always a physical opponent in front of you in kumite, whereas in kata you are performing alone. But you have to keep in mind that there is someone in front of you when you do kata too! So kumite has really helped adjust my kata based on that conception."

### On Strength Training
"Great technique in kata can only be achieved with having a solid foundation of strength."

### On Mental Preparation
"To be able to perform your best at a tournament, it is important to simply act as normal as possible. And for that, you need to be very mentally focused during regular training time."

### On Goals
"Everyone has a goal. Whether that is to become a world champion or not doesn't really matter. Although your goal may often look hard to achieve at first, if you continually make efforts toward your goal, then your time, energy and commitment will never have been wasted. Stay strong until you win against yourself."

## Thread Connections

- **Thread 2 (The Way Is in Training):** Her 7-year journey from first tournament to first win. The 5-hour train rides. "Stay strong until you win against yourself."
- **Thread 3 (Hard Conversations):** Inoue told her what she needed to hear, pushed her limits.
- **Thread 5 (Mushin):** "Act as normal as possible" in competition = the state beyond self-consciousness.
- **Thread 11 (Okinawan vs. Modern):** Started Goju-ryu, moved to Shito-ryu, bridging Okinawan and Japanese traditions. "Don't consider traditional and contemporary as separate."
- **Thread 12 (Kata as expression):** Her Chatanyara Kushanku as the ultimate expression of lineage: Hayashi → Inoue → Usami. The kata was introduced to the world stage by Inoue's earlier student Hisami Yokoyama.
- **Thread 14 (Ego and AI):** Retired at 27 — having achieved her goal, she moved on rather than clinging to identity as competitor. Non-attachment to outcomes.
- **Lineage:** Goju-ryu (childhood) → Shito-ryu under Inoue → Inoue-ha Shito-ryu Keishin-kai. Through Inoue: Hayashi → Inoue → Usami. The Chatanyara Kushanku lineage: Yokoyama (1994 worlds) → Usami (2012 worlds) — both Inoue students.
